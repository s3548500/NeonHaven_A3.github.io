Group Reflection

Kelvin Tran

1. What went well?

All work was managed carefully both in the report and in the game project.

2. What could be improved?

I should distribute the work more equally next time.

3. At least one thing that was surprising?

Each member has their own strong resolve to complete the work presented to them.

4. At least one thing that you have learned about groups.

To meet often with the group as each member has their own perspective of when they’re busy.

Alex Strates

1. What went well?

Everyone shared the same passion for the project and was happy to work together creatively and positively

2. What could be improved?

The work load and work schedule had not improved from lads assignment and this the same problems occurred.

3. At least one thing that was surprising?

How we managed to create a creative outcome in the end and how all of our different ideas contributed to the final product

4. At least one thing that you have learned about groups.

When clear goals are highlighted it is most important to take immediate action on these to help the group succeed.

Kemal Dogru

1. What went well?

The development of the game went well under the time limitations we had, I expected us to have barely any progress done. The game will have a fully functonal prototype for the presentation due Friday.

2. What could be improved?

Everyone just needs to communicate and attend meetings together, this little act can solve all the groups problems.

3. At least one thing that was surprising?

The development of the game was very surprising as I was expecting us to fail and not make any progress, but thanks to Kelvins expertise we got the work done.

4. At least one thing that you have learned about groups.

Groups are hard to function when no one is passionate or dejected about the project. I believe as the project seemed “difficult” and “impossible”, some group members lost the passion and drive to work on the project, so they stopped caring.
